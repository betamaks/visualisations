How a Canine Heart Works

One looping beat that ties together the two circulatory loops, the four valves, and the electrical trigger that turns a quiet filling heart into a forward pump.

Big Picture

The right side of the heart sends oxygen-poor blood to the lungs. The left side sends oxygen-rich blood to the body. Valves only open when pressure behind them becomes higher than pressure in front of them.

Electrical start The SA node in the right atrium starts each normal beat.
AV node delay A brief pause lets the atria top off the ventricles before systole.
Blue route Right-heart circulation carries oxygen-poor blood toward the lungs.
Red route Left-heart circulation carries oxygen-rich blood toward the body.

Current Step

Diastole

Passive filling

Relaxed ventricles stay below atrial pressure, so return from body and lungs flows into both ventricles.

  • Blood path: return from body and lungs refills the atria, then the ventricles.
  • Valve state: tricuspid + mitral open; pulmonic + aortic closed.
  • Pressure rule: atria and veins are above relaxed ventricles.
  • Electrical cue: the next impulse is about to start at the SA node.
  • Heart sound: quiet filling between S2 and the next S1.

One Beat

Phase 1

Passive filling

Most ventricular filling happens while both ventricles are relaxed.

Phase 2

Atrial kick

Atrial contraction tops off the ventricles just before systole.

Phase 3

Pressure build / S1

AV valves close and all valves are briefly shut while pressure rises.

Phase 4

Ejection

Semilunar valves open and blood leaves for lungs and body.

Phase 5

Relaxation / S2

Semilunar valves close, atria refill, and the next fill begins.
