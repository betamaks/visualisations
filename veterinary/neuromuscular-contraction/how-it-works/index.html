Step Through The Normal Sequence
Reading Guide

Gold follows the electrical handoff, teal marks acetylcholine in the cleft, blue tracks calcium-driven coupling, and the red overlays show where common disorders interrupt the sequence.

Normal logic Motor neuron depolarization opens presynaptic calcium channels, releases acetylcholine, launches a muscle action potential, then triggers sarcoplasmic reticulum calcium release.
Electrolytes Potassium shifts mostly change membrane excitability. Calcium imbalance can distort threshold behavior and the reliability of contraction, with species-specific clinical patterns.
Neuromuscular disorders Botulism and tick paralysis block presynaptic handoff. Myasthenia gravis removes receptor reserve at the end-plate.
Species note This plate uses a generic veterinary motor unit. The core sequence is conserved, but the clinical phenotype varies between dogs, cats, horses, cattle, and other species.
