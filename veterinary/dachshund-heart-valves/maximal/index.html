Maximal Study Visual

Four repeatable phase panels plus one anatomy reference and one live summary.

1. Ventricular filling

Diastolic inflow

The relaxed ventricles are low pressure spaces, so both AV valves are open and blood falls in from the atria.

2. AV valve closure

S1

Ventricular pressure starts rising. The tricuspid and mitral valves shut first, creating the S1 heart sound.

3. Ejection

Forward outflow

Once ventricular pressure exceeds pulmonary artery and aortic pressure, the semilunar valves open and blood is ejected.

4. Semilunar closure

S2

As the ventricles relax, pressure in the great vessels becomes higher than ventricular pressure. The exit valves snap shut, producing S2.

Anatomy Orientation

Orientation only. Valve markers stay neutral here so the active state is read from the highlighted panel.

Active Phase

Ventricular filling

Atrial pressure is higher than ventricular pressure, so blood passes through the open tricuspid and mitral valves.

  • AV valves: open
  • Semilunar valves: closed
  • Pressure rule: atria > ventricles
